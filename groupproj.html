Group Projects

INTRO WEEK

In the first week of the 3rd semester, we kicked off with an intro week, forming groups and tackling various tasks. One task involved designing a business card to represent ourselves. Research revealed that business cards usually have these key elements: name/logo, contact info, and possibly location and social media. My card incorporated these essentials in a simple blue and white design initially. In the second iteration, I experimented with black and gold for a touch of luxury and professionalism . For the front, I created a temporary logo using the first letters of my name, with a curved watermark outside for visual interest. Nova Cut font was ideal, and an overlay box added uniqueness. The back used Garamond for a professional look. A gold bookmark icon symbolizes my commitment to organization, aiming to provide clients with streamlined processes and easy access to valuable resources. see cards

TRIPLE-S

During the group formation, we decided on the name TRIPLE-s to signify our third semester and our focus on strategic research, swift development, and stylish designs. Our initial step as a group was to draft a contract agreement, outlining our agreed-upon rules. Following this, we delved into brainstorming sessions to define our brand identity, starting with individual iterations of our logo. My approach involved researching companies using the letter "s" in their logos, studying fonts, shapes, and structures. After creating numerous iterations, we sought feedback on each design. Ultimately, the version crafted by Danya resonated the most, leading us to refine variations based on that design. Upon presenting our branding concept to fellow students, feedback favored the final "fan-like" logo over other options. Some perceived the initial snake-themed logo as conveying a pharmaceutical or deceptive vibe. Consequently, we chose to further enhance the fan-like logo, resulting in our finalized logo design. You can view the iterations here.

After we designed the logo, our next step was defining our brand identity. We discussed what our brand should represent and settled on the color purple. Then, we decided on the services we would offer. I created visual representations of our branding three times. The first version was deemed too professional after we sought feedback. So, I made a second iteration, which received positive feedback but needed some tweaks. Upon personal reflection, I felt the visuals were too playful. Hence, I created one more iteration to find a balance between professionalism and playfulness. You can find the iterations here
