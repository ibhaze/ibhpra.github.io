INTRO WEEK

During the introductory week of the 3rd semester, our group had a fun task: designing personal business cards. It was a chance to get creative and show off our design skills. For me, this project was more than just making a card with my details. It was about learning and showing what I could do. I started with a simple design in blue and white. It had my name, logo, and contact info. After that, I asked for feedback from a teacher in semester 2, and he said I should make it represent me. So, I started working on a different design with a black background. This design also includes a bookmark icon. I chose black, grey, and gold as my personal brand colors because they reflect my personality and style. Black represents sophistication and elegance, while grey symbolizes professionalism and neutrality. Gold signifies luxury and prestige. Together, these colors convey a sense of sophistication and professionalism that aligns with my personal brand. As for the bookmark icon, I incorporated it into the design to symbolize my commitment to organization and efficiency. Just as a bookmark helps keep things organized in a book, I strive to keep my work and projects well-structured and easily accessible. Therefore, the bookmark icon serves as a visual representation of this aspect of my personality and work ethic. But I didn't stop there. I wanted to make my card look fancy, so I tried adding black and gold. This showed I could make interactive things, which shows proficiency in Learning Outcome 1. In the end, my business card wasn't just a piece of paper. It was a way to show who I am as a designer and what I've learned. see cards .

PROJECT

Our project for the semester involved creating a personalized nutrition plan for users utilizing generative AI. Our client, Livewall, is deeply immersed in the world of generative AI, presenting an excellent opportunity for me to enhance my skills in this domain. Using the double diamond method We initiated the project by conducting primary research on user preferences in nutrition apps or websites and formulated a survey based on the findings. We also utilized the Lotus Framework as a brainstorming tool. I placed various words related to the main goal of creating a nutrition plan and branched out from there. This framework was crucial for us because it enabled us to explore the realm of nutrition plans more comprehensively and delve deeper into the subject.. Our research indicated that users primarily seek nutrition plans to maintain their health or achieve weight loss goals. Subsequently, I refined the survey and targeted it towards our client's demographic, which comprises middle-aged individuals. Following this, I solicited feedback from Penny , who suggested improvements to the survey. These included incorporating an introductory section to clarify the concept of a nutrition plan for users and integrating a question addressing their personal preferences, such as weight loss or muscle gain. I then implemented her feedback and showed her the improvements and she said the questions were good . .These are the results . After multiple iterations of the survey , I created a concept document to serve as a guide for the primary and secondary research , and I was again in charge of creating the POV /HMV to kind of serve as a way of viewing the product and users from a different perspective , then I asked from feedback from pennny and she liked the fact that I had a lot of scenarios in the HMW section. After this , I created interview questions to have more quality and useful insights , I refined the interview questions after asking for feedback from a semester 2 Teacher and he said it'll be great to split the questions for clarity and organization of the questions and so that it'll be easier when asking a certain line of questions to the user so that follow up questions will have more context . .From the feedback I reflected and decided to split the questions into two sections ..You can find the interview questions and transcript here. I also created the low fidelity prototype for the nutrition plan on my own so that we'd have a diffferent variation of the one we created together . Find the prototype here. In this section of my portfolio , I displayed my proficiency in LO3 by iterating and improving the survey and interview based on the feedback I got from the teachers and I displayed LO4 by conducting research to get to know more about the other nutrition apps and websites and their components through the double diamond research tool and the lotus framework . I also displayed LO5 by creating my own version of the low-fidelity prototype to give my group different options .I displayed my proficiency in LO1 by creating a low-fi prototype , using the double diamond method , creating the concept document and creating interview questions .

We started the mid-fidelity prototyping phase, and I suggested that we create a mobile version. I was handed the task of creating that, and so I did, although from the start, I didn't agree with a lot of design choices and the fact that we had such a little number of questions to ask the users. During the creation of our prototype, I noticed a usability problem: in the question section, there was no back or next button. So, if a user clicked an answer, it would automatically take the user to the next page, and the user wouldn't be able to go back. After I noticed the problem, I told my teammates, and I fixed it. After I created my prototype, I got feedback from Penny and she said that I should think of the branding for the nutrition plan app and not just use Livewall's image , she also said that I should take the fact that people don't really have time to cook for breakfast into account and after thinking about it for a bit I remembered that the plan was going to be created by AI and the one I had was just a makeshift and so I informed her.
