INTRO WEEK

Goal

During the introductory week of the 3rd semester, our group had a fun task: designing personal business cards. It was a chance to get creative and show off our design skills. For me, this project was more than just making a card with my details. It was about learning and showing what I could do.

Process

I started with a simple design in blue and white. It had my name, logo, and contact info. After that, I asked for feedback from a teacher in semester 2, and he said I should make it represent me. So, I started working on a different design with a black background. This design also includes a bookmark icon.

I chose black, grey, and gold as my personal brand colors because they reflect my personality and style. Black represents sophistication and elegance, while grey symbolizes professionalism and neutrality. Gold signifies luxury and prestige. Together, these colors convey a sense of sophistication and professionalism that aligns with my personal brand. As for the bookmark icon, I incorporated it into the design to symbolize my commitment to organization and efficiency. Just as a bookmark helps keep things organized in a book, I strive to keep my work and projects well-structured and easily accessible. Therefore, the bookmark icon serves as a visual representation of this aspect of my personality and work ethic.

Reflection

But I didn't stop there. I wanted to make my card look fancy, so I tried adding black and gold. This showed I could make interactive things, which demonstrates proficiency in Learning Outcome 1. In the end, my business card wasn't just a piece of paper. It was a way to show who I am as a designer and what I've learned. See cards.

During our group formation, we decided to call ourselves TRIPLE-S. This name represented us as third-semester students and highlighted our focus on strategic research, quick development, and cool designs. To start off, we had to creat a contract agreement to set up our rules.

PROJECT

Overview

Our project for the semester involved creating a personalized nutrition plan for users utilizing generative AI. Our client, Livewall, is deeply immersed in the world of generative AI, presenting an excellent opportunity for me to enhance my skills in this domain.

Research and Brainstorming

Using the double diamond method, I initiated the project by conducting primary research on user preferences in nutrition apps or websites and formulated a survey based on the findings. I also utilized the Lotus Framework as a brainstorming tool. I placed various words related to the main goal of creating a nutrition plan and branched out from there. This framework was crucial for us because it enabled us to explore the realm of nutrition plans more comprehensively and delve deeper into the subject. Our research indicated that users primarily seek nutrition plans to maintain their health or achieve weight loss goals.

Survey Development

I refined the survey and targeted it towards our client's demographic, which comprises middle-aged individuals. Following this, I solicited feedback from Penny, who suggested improvements to the survey. These included incorporating an introductory section to clarify the concept of a nutrition plan for users and integrating a question addressing their personal preferences, such as weight loss or muscle gain. I then implemented her feedback and showed her the improvements, and she said the questions were good. You can see the results.

Concept Document and POV/HMW

After multiple iterations of the survey, I created a concept document to serve as a guide for the primary and secondary research. I was in charge of creating the POV/HMW to view the product and users from different perspectives. I asked for feedback from Penny, and she liked the fact that I had a lot of scenarios in the HMW section.

Interview Process

I created interview questions to gain more quality and useful insights. After asking for feedback from a semester 2 teacher, he suggested splitting the questions for clarity and organization. This would make it easier when asking a certain line of questions to the user, so follow-up questions would have more context. Reflecting on the feedback, I decided to split the questions into two sections. You can find the interview questions and transcript here.

Prototype Development

I created the low-fidelity prototype for the nutrition plan on my own to provide a different variation of the one we created together. You can find the prototype here.

Reflection

In this project, I demonstrated proficiency in several learning outcomes:

  • LO1: I created a low-fi prototype, used the double diamond method, created the concept document, and formulated interview questions.
  • LO3: I iterated and improved the survey and interview based on the feedback I received from the teachers.
  • LO4: I conducted research to learn more about other nutrition apps and websites and their components through the double diamond research tool and the Lotus Framework.
  • LO5: I created my own version of the low-fidelity prototype to give my group different options.
During the course of the project I learnt a lot about frameworks and how to use them

PROJECT: MAIN PHASE

Mid-Fidelity Prototyping

We started the mid-fidelity prototyping phase, and I suggested creating a mobile version. I was tasked with this, and despite some initial disagreements about design choices and the limited number of questions to ask users, I proceeded. During the prototype creation, I identified a usability problem: the question section lacked back and next buttons, causing users to automatically move to the next page without the ability to go back. I informed my teammates about this issue and fixed it.

Feedback and Iterations

After completing the prototype, I received feedback from Penny. She advised me to consider the branding for the nutrition plan app and not just use Livewall's image. She also pointed out that people often don't have time to cook for breakfast, which I took into account, remembering that the plan would be generated by AI and my version was a placeholder. You can view the prototype here.

Applying Figma Knowledge

For this prototype, I applied the knowledge from the Figma tutorial I took, ensuring a smooth layout through the use of gridlines. After the first phase, I decided to conduct user testing before continuing the prototyping phase. I asked Penny and my classmates to try the app. They suggested that branding the nutrition plan with its own identity, rather than using Livewall's logo, would be better. They also noted some usability issues: Penny thought using 'START' as a button to enter the plan was a bad idea, and green was a poor choice for the button color. Lars suggested having a visible scrollbar would be useful.

Improvements Based on Feedback

Taking their feedback into account, I made changes to the branding, button color, and text within the button on the "Mid-fid prototype 2nd iteration" page. I then started working on the nutrition plan page. Although we had a design plan, I felt it was not good enough. Instead of making large-scale changes, I made small adjustments that improved the appearance of the nutrition plan, such as adding borders around each day's plan. After receiving feedback from another student, who found my version easier on the eyes and suggested adding dates to each week for tracking progress, I implemented these changes.After making the change to the border , my teammates disagreed with the use of the borders and decided to go on without them . The next step was to create the website after the mid-fidelity prototype and I started off by creating the page for the plan itself and the layout for the pages .After creating the main nutrition plan page , I made the design responsive making the waves appear like as if they're in motion by applying transitioning colour changes and after that I asked for feedback from my peers and they said it'll be better for the user if the timing between the colour changes were longer so that the users don't get distracted by them and so I implemented this . After this phase , I asked the client for a meeting to see our progress and he was a bit dissapointed about the fact that he didnt have much involvement in the earlier stages of the project he noted that he wasn't carried along ,and that he expected us to carry him along and not just work alone after him giving us a 15 minute presentation ...My mistake was not being proactive in these things and expecting the leader of the group to take care of communcication with the client as agreed in the contract .. although he was impressed with the amount of effort we put into the research he told us too find a connection between the research and our decisions in the project .

Conclusion and Reflection

A couple of days leading up to the deadline , I applied a lot of the technical feedback that we got from Bas(our client) including adding a reminder button to the prototype even though it's not in the final version , I also spaced out the text on the main plan page like he told me to .. showing my proficiency in learning outcome 3 by using feedback to come up with another iteration .
