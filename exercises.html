PASSION PROJECT

For a class project, we were asked to come up with a passion project. I spent some time brainstorming ideas until I landed on something I truly cared about: a Community Connection Platform for Students. I envisioned a space where college students, aged 18-25, could come together, share resources, and support one another. The idea behind this platform was simple yet powerful. I wanted to address common challenges faced by students, such as stress, isolation, and academic pressure, by creating a sense of belonging and community. So, I set out to develop a virtual hub accessible via smartphones or computers, where students could connect with peers, join interest-based groups, and access various resources—from study guides to mental health support services. As I worked on this project, I realized it wasn't just about creating a platform; it was about creating an environment for personal and academic growth. I wanted students to feel empowered, supported, and encouraged to reach their full potential. To start the project , I spent time observing students in various settings—libraries, cafeterias, and study groups. This allowed me to observe their interactions, communication patterns, and technological preferences firsthand. I found out that most students exhibited a high reliance on smartphones . They frequently checked their devices for messages, social media updates, and academic resources. I also noticed that a lot of students practiced eye-blocking which is very common in people who have social anxiety . This was how this project started . Through this journey, I demonstrated my ability to conceptualize and develop interactive media products (LO1). But it wasn't just about the technical aspects; I also considered important factors like ethics, culture, and sustainability (LO4). Moreover, this project highlighted my commitment to self-awareness and continuous learning (LO5), as I strived to understand my strengths and weaknesses while working towards a common goal: making a positive impact on student communities.

INTERACTIVE DESIGN

Becoming a UX designer is my goal, and Penny's lectures have really helped me understand the field. In one class, Penny gave us an exercise to create a prototype for a webshop's favorite page and map out how users would navigate it. This exercise taught me a lot. As I worked on the prototype, I learned about user flows and why it's important to think about different scenarios, even the negative ones. In the first try, I missed a page in the flow. Penny told me to think about situations where users might only want to checkout with one item from the favorite page. So, I added checkboxes for each item, making it possible for users to checkout with just one item. I kept asking for feedback, and Penny suggested I make the link between the favorite and checkout pages clearer. So, I added a confirmation pop-up to bridge the gap between the two pages, making it easier for users to navigate. In Penny's class, I also learned about the multi-modal wizard. I wanted to use it for my project, but my teammates weren't on board. Despite this, I showed my ability to create design media products by making a low-fidelity prototype displaying my proficiency in learning outcome 1 . I also showed I could take feedback and make changes, which is important for learning , displaying my proficiency in learning outcome 3. This whole experience has taught me a lot and helped me grow as a designer. Penny's guidance and the supportive class environment have been really valuable.
