BRANDING EXERCISE

Goal

In a User-Centered Design (UCD) class, I had to create a brand based on a given word. I was given the word "Food". I decided to create an app called "Discount Digest", where people could compare prices and quality from different stores. I picked the name because it shows my focus on finding good deals and gathering information. "Digest" means collecting information, while "Discount" shows that the app helps people find cheaper stuff.

Process

I started by thinking of food-related words and what they mean. Then, I came up with lots of ideas and wrote a story to explain why the app is useful. This matches Learning Outcome 1 (LO1) because I came up with ideas for a digital product and made it real.

To make our app better, I asked for feedback from my classmates, and they told me I should find ways to get stores to join the app and think about new ways to collect items from different stores. After their feedback, I worked on improving the branding and had an idea to have an Uber-like system for the delivery where the drivers closest to the users take their orders and go to the most affordable shop to get the order.

Reflection

This project showed that I understand how to design things with users in mind and how to come up with new ideas. I also showed professionalism by thinking about real-world problems and finding ways to make my idea work for everyone.

PASSION PROJECT

Goal

For a class project, we were asked to come up with a passion project. I spent some time brainstorming ideas until I landed on something I truly cared about: a Community Connection Platform for Students. I envisioned a space where college students, aged 18-25, could come together, share resources, and support one another.

Process

The idea behind this platform was simple yet powerful. I wanted to address common challenges faced by students, such as stress, isolation, and academic pressure, by creating a sense of belonging and community. So, I set out to develop a virtual hub accessible via smartphones or computers, where students could connect with peers, join interest-based groups, and access various resources—from study guides to mental health support services.

As I worked on this project, I realized it wasn't just about creating a platform; it was about creating an environment for personal and academic growth. I wanted students to feel empowered, supported, and encouraged to reach their full potential. To start the project, I spent time observing students in various settings—libraries, cafeterias, and study groups. This allowed me to observe their interactions, communication patterns, and technological preferences firsthand. I found out that most students exhibited a high reliance on smartphones. They frequently checked their devices for messages, social media updates, and academic resources. I also noticed that a lot of students practiced eye-blocking, which is very common in people who have social anxiety. This was how this project started.

Reflection

Through this journey, I demonstrated my ability to conceptualize and develop interactive media products (LO1). But it wasn't just about the technical aspects; I also considered important factors like ethics, culture, and sustainability (LO4). Moreover, this project highlighted my commitment to self-awareness and continuous learning (LO5), as I strived to understand my strengths and weaknesses while working towards a common goal: making a positive impact on student communities.

POV/HOW MIGHT WE

Goal

We were given an assignment to create a POV/How Might We document to demonstrate our approach to research. For this assignment, I aimed to understand the essential elements of a POV document and apply this knowledge to my passion project by seeing through the eyes of the user.

Process

I began by conducting primary research to learn what a POV document should display. I then created a POV for my passion project to understand the user perspective. After completing my initial draft, I sought feedback from Penny. She provided valuable suggestions, advising me to avoid being too specific and to clearly define what users will be doing in the scenarios. I applied her feedback and improved upon my previous iteration. In my second iteration, each scenario was less specific, reflecting her advice. After presenting the revised document to Penny, she appreciated that I created more POVs and showed improvement based on her feedback. You can find the document here.

Reflection

This exercise was instrumental in showcasing my proficiency in Learning Outcome 3 (LO3) by actively seeking feedback and iterating on my work. Additionally, I demonstrated Learning Outcome 4 (LO4) by applying a part of the Double Diamond research method through my research on the essential components of a POV document. This project highlighted my ability to integrate feedback effectively, improve my work iteratively, and employ research methodologies to enhance the quality of my outputs. Overall, it reinforced the importance of user-centered design and the continuous refinement of ideas to better meet user needs.

INTERACTIVE DESIGN

Goal

Becoming a UX designer is my goal, and Penny's lectures have really helped me understand the field. In one class, Penny gave us an exercise to create a prototype for a webshop's favorite page and map out how users would navigate it. This exercise taught me a lot.

Process

As I worked on the prototype, I learned about user flows and why it's important to think about different scenarios, even the negative ones. In the first try, I missed a page in the flow. Penny told me to think about situations where users might only want to checkout with one item from the favorite page. So, I added checkboxes for each item, making it possible for users to checkout with just one item.

I kept asking for feedback, and Penny suggested I make the link between the favorite and checkout pages clearer. So, I added a confirmation pop-up to bridge the gap between the two pages, making it easier for users to navigate.

In Penny's class, I also learned about the multi-modal wizard. I wanted to use it for my project, but my teammates weren't on board. Despite this, I showed my ability to create design media products by making a low-fidelity prototype displaying my proficiency in learning outcome 1. I also showed I could take feedback and make changes, which is important for learning, displaying my proficiency in learning outcome 3.

Reflection

This whole experience has taught me a lot and helped me grow as a designer. Penny's guidance and the supportive class environment have been really valuable.
